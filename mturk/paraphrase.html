Question 1

Are all these questions asking the same thing? (It is okay if they are negations of each other.)

  • Should the government provide a path to citizenship for undocumented immigrants currently living in the country?
  • Do you believe the government should facilitate a way to citizenship for undocumented immigrants who are already living in the country?
  • Should a pathway to citizenship be provided by the government for undocumented immigrants who are currently in the country?
  • Is it the government's responsibility to offer a route to citizenship for undocumented immigrants residing in the country?
  • Is it appropriate for the government to establish a path to citizenship for undocumented immigrants living in the country at present?

Explanation: The option "Is it the government's responsibility to offer a route to citizenship for undocumented immigrants residing in the country?" makes note of "responsibility" which may be interpreted factually as opposed to as a matter of preference. For example, the goverment may not IN FACT be responsible but we may think that they should be responsible. This contrasts with the other questions.

Question 2

Are all these questions asking the same thing? (It is okay if they are negations of each other.)

  • Do you support mandatory vaccination policies?
  • Are you in favor of compulsory vaccination policies?
  • Do you advocate for the implementation of mandatory vaccination policies?
  • Is it your belief that vaccination policies should be mandatory?
  • Do you back the enforcement of mandatory vaccination policies?

Explanation: These all mean the same thing.

Question 3

Are all these questions asking the same thing? (It is okay if they are negations of each other.)

  • In your view, should we protect religious freedom even if it may cause discrimination?
  • Do you hold the belief that even if it may cause discrimination, religious freedom should still be protected?
  • Do you think that the protection of religious freedom should be upheld even if it might result in discrimination?
  • Do you believe that religious freedom should be protected even if it potentially leads to discrimination?
  • Should religious freedom be safeguarded even if it could possibly result in discrimination, in your opinion?

Explanation: These all mean the same thing.

Question 4

Are all these questions asking the same thing? (It is okay if they are negations of each other.)

  • Is it your belief that all internet traffic should be handled without discrimination?
  • Do you believe that all internet traffic should be treated equally?
  • Do you think that all internet traffic should receive equal treatment?
  • Do you agree with the principle of equal treatment for all internet traffic?

Explanation: The question, "Is it your belief that all internet traffic should be handled without discrimination?" uses the term "discrimination" while the rest simly mention "equality". A lack of discrimination, under some view, might not be the same thing as equality.
